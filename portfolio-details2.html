Kruger National Park: A Biodiversity Hotspo Kruger National Park, one of Africa’s largest game reserves, is a sanctuary of African wildlife and is nestled in the heart of South Africa. Spanning across nearly two million hectares, this park is not only vast in size but also in its biodiversity. Established in 1898 to protect the wildlife of the South African Lowveld, this national park is now part of the Kruger to Canyons Biosphere, an area designated by UNESCO as an International Man and Biosphere Reserve.

Preservation and TourismKruger National Park is renowned for offering some of the best wildlife-viewing opportunities in the world. It is home to the famous Big Five: lions, leopards, rhinos, elephants, and buffalos. The park's extensive road network and numerous hides provide visitors with close-up encounters in a natural setting, making it a photographer's and wildlife enthusiast's paradise.
